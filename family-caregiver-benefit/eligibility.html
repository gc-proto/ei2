Step 2. Eligibility

The information provided below should be used as a guideline. We encourage you to apply for benefits and allow your Service Canada representative to determine if you are eligible.

To be eligible to establish a claim for the Benefit, you must be able to meet the following conditions:

  • Your regular weekly earnings from work have decreased by more than 40% because you need to provide care or support to the critically ill or injured adult
  • You accumulated 600 insured hours of work in the 52 weeks before the start of your claim, or since the start of your last claim, whichever is shorter
  • You are a family member of the critically ill or injured adult, or they consider you to be like a family member
  • The person is at least 18 years old when a medical doctor or nurse practitioner certifies that they are critically ill or injured

If you are self-employed

You may be eligible for the Benefit if you registered for access to EI special benefits for self-employed people and waited 12 months from the date of your confirmed registration. You must also meet all of the following conditions:

  • The amount of time you spend on your business has decreased by more than 40% because you need to provide care or support to the critically ill or injured adult
  • You have earned a minimum amount of self-employed earnings during the calendar year before the year you submit your claim. This amount changes from year to year. For example, to apply for benefits in 2017, you need to have earned at least $6,888 in 2016
  • You are a family member of the critically ill or injured adult, or they consider you to be like a family member
  • The person is at least 18 years old when a medical doctor or nurse practitioner certifies that they are critically ill or injured

Care or support to a critically ill or injured adult living outside Canada

If you leave Canada to provide care or support to a critically ill or injured adult, you may still be eligible to receive the Benefit. You must submit an application and supporting documents with the same type of proof that is required for a critically ill or injured adult living in Canada.

How much I will receive?

Service Canada cannot tell you exactly how much you will receive until your application is processed. For most people, the basic rate used to calculate EI benefits is 55% of average insurable weekly earnings, up to a maximum amount. In 2017, the maximum amount is $51,300 or $543 a week.

You could receive up to 15 weeks of benefits in the 52 weeks following the date the adult is certified to be critically ill or injured. The payments can be taken all at once or in separate periods.
